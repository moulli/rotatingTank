Welcome to DOTTER

The goal for you is to pin points on the fish, that is freely swimming in a rotating tank. This annoted dataset will help train a convolutional neural network to get the position of the fish inside the tank on a few particular points.

In order to get the position of the fish, an image will be displayed on the whole width of your browser. You will be asked to pin 5 different points on the fish. After selecting these 5 points one after the other, the next image will automatically be displayed, randomly from a list of all the images remaining. We have 252 videos of 6,000 frames, so that is a total of 1,512,000 images, a lot of them. The more images labelled, the best. As we will rotate images in the training set, each labelled image will count for 4 images in the set!
Here are the points that you need to pick, IN THE EXACT ORDER:
  • the left eye 1
  • the right eye 2
  • the body center 3
  • the mid-tail 4
  • the tail end 5
NB: Consider that the top of the fish is always facing you
If you think you made a mistake on the image you just labelled, you can always get back to it by scrolling to the bottom of the page, and selecting 'Oops back to previous image'. You can only do it once, so it is impossible to label an image you did two frames before!
Have fun!
